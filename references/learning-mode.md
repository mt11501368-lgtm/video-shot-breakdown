# Learning analysis mode

Answer: **Why is this effective?**

For every shot, identify:

1. Narrative function: new information, escalation, setup, payoff, reaction, transition, or hook.
2. Visual method: shot size, composition, depth, blocking, light, color, and camera movement.
3. Edit logic: cut trigger, pace change, visual match, sound lead, or contrast.
4. Sound role: dialogue priority, ambience, music intensity, silence, and effect accents.
5. Emotional effect: what the viewer should feel before and after the shot.
6. Why it works in this specific context.
7. Transferable formula stated without copying characters or plot details.

Required workbook sheets:

- `学习拆解说明`
- `学习拉片表`
- `结构总结`
- `技巧清单`

Quality bar:

- Ground every claim in a visible or audible detail.
- Express formulas as reusable cause-and-effect patterns.
- Separate a technique from its example.
- Include the opening hook, overall emotional curve, and ending hook.
- Note weaknesses such as continuity drift, jump-axis edits, redundant microcuts, or unclear identity.
